Rooted in a background in botanical studies, Uwaya completed her BSc at Trent University. After attending Bastyr University for her first year of naturopathic medical school, Uwaya completed her diploma and board exams in Naturopathic Medicine following three years at Canadian College of Naturopathic Medicine. She has been practicing in Peterborough area since 1999. She has also been a teacher of botanical medicine to health professionals, and studied classical homeopathy for 8 years post-graduate with Hahnemann Instituut in Holland.
Following the principles of naturopathic medicine, Uwaya’s aim is to help people attain quality of life through healthy living. She believes that this has a ripple effect and contributes to healthy families and communities. She is a general practitioner of naturopathic medicine.

As a Naturopathic Doctor, Susan believes that patient education is one of the most important factors in sustaining good health and nurturing an awareness of a healthy lifestyle. “I believe that each patient must be treated as an individual. My role is to empower people to take control of their health, and I feel this is a key step in becoming and remaining healthy. She also loves working with children.
Susan Joyce is an active member of the Ontario Association of Naturopathic Doctors (OAND) and the Canadian Association of Naturopathic Doctors (CAND).

WHAT IS NATUROPATHIC MEDICINE?

Naturopathic medicine is a comprehensive and holistic approach to improving health and treating illness in patients of all ages. It is the art and science of disease diagnosis, treatment and prevention using natural therapies and substances. Naturopathic treatments may be used as an alternative to allopathic (regular) medicine or can complement conventional medical treatments. The aim of the Naturopathic Doctor is always to support and stimulate the body’s ability to heal itself.


NATUROPATHIC MEDICAL TRAINING

Naturopathic Doctors receive a minimum of seven years of specialized study. After three years of premedical university education, they must complete an intensive four year naturopathic medical program at a recognized college of naturopathic medicine. In addition to 1500 hours of supervised clinical experience, graduates must also pass rigorous North American and provincial licensing exams before they are registered to practice as doctors of Naturopathic Medicine in Ontario.
